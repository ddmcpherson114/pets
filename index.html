Birds

Birds are warm-blooded, egg-laying, feathered vertebrates (class Aves) evolved from theropod dinosaurs, characterized by beaks, lightweight skeletons, and four-chambered hearts.

Fish

Fish are cold-blooded vertebrates that live in water, breathe using gills, and typically possess scales and fins, with over 30,000 known species adapting to habitats ranging from deep oceans to freshwater rivers.

Lizards

Lizards are a diverse group of over 6,000 species of reptiles found worldwide (except Antarctica), characterized by dry, scaly skin, four limbs (with exceptions), and, unlike snakes, movable eyelids.

Tarantulas (family Theraphosidae) are large, hairy, long-lived spiders found in warm regions globally, known for being nocturnal burrowers.
